Copper thin film (~50 nm) on a titanium adhesion layer (~5 nm) on a silicon substrate, measured in THF.
There is a copper oxide (~3 nm) on top of the copper (THF side).

The incoming beam is coming from the silicon side. The copper and titanium SLD can be considered fixed.
The copper oxide thickness, roughness and SLD are uncertain.
The THF could be either protonated or deuterated.

Find the optimal deuteration ratio of the THF to maximise sensitivity
to changes in the copper oxide thickness, roughness, and SLD.
Evaluate the deuteration ratio between 0 and 1 in steps of 20%.
